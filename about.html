What We Do

The UIC Writing Center provides one-to-one writing support to all UIC students, regardless of background or discipline, by experienced peer tutors, in two forms of writing assistance: Technical or Substantive.

For students seeking technical help, tutors work with students on individual papers in order to point out sentence-level structural errors. Tutors may:

  • Spot patterns of grammatical and word choice (compositional) errors in a student's prose;
  • Teach how to identify and correct these patterns;
  • Make suggestions for how to revise for greater clarity and coherence

For students seeking substantive help, tutors work with students on individual papers in order to develop and improve academic argumentation and structure. Tutors may:

  • Advise how to construct an argument;
  • Comment on argumentation and use of textual evidence;
  • Make suggestions to polish organization and structure

Please note that, for the sake of academic integrity, tutors do not:

  • Teach course texts or content;
  • Copy-edit or “fix” students writings;
  • Offer “answers” to writing assignments;
  • Provide assistance with take-home exams

While tutors can discuss text/content insofar as it pertains to improving a paper, all text/content should be discussed with course instructors. Tutors can help students to see frequently made mistakes and self-correct, but cannot revise or re-write student work to be grammatically correct; likewise, tutors can suggest ways to approach an assignment, but cannot tell students what to write about.

To ensure a productive tutoring session, students should bring:

  • A draft or outline;
  • The paper assignment;
  • Clear questions (e.g., What do I want to get out of the tutoring session? Is there a particular idea/paragraph/point that I am concerned about? Is there a particular area of writing with which I need help, such as thesis, introduction, thesis, evidence?)
